Who Am I?

I'm Raj, a passionate Data Scientist with a background in Physics. My academic journey culminated with a Doctorate in Soft Matter Physics from Syracuse University, a path where I honed my expertise in both computational and experimental methodologies. My doctoral research was centered around the intricate study of non-linear deformation in thin films, a topic that perfectly married my love for intricate physical phenomena with advanced computational analysis.

My academic pursuits have been more than just a path to a degree; they represent my unwavering passion for knowledge and an endless fascination with the intricate dance of data and algorithms. As a Data Scientist, I bring a unique perspective shaped by my physics background, allowing me to approach data with a blend of scientific rigor and innovative thinking.

My aim is to continually evolve in the dynamic world of data science, exploring new horizons and contributing to the field with both depth and breadth of knowledge.

Education

Doctorate Degree of Physics

Excerpts from my research projects:

  • Simulation Optimization: Crafted and refined an analytical mesh model to optimize the energetics of floating thin films. Utilized the conjugate gradient descent algorithm, achieving a significant reduction in simulation runtime by over 40%.
  • Computational Collaboration: Engaged in interdisciplinary work, merging computational methods with experimental insights. Employed interface surface triangulation and meshing packages in Python and C, collaborating with experimental researchers to quantify critical physical parameters and translate experimental data into simulation-ready formats.
  • Data Analysis & Model Development: Spearheaded the development of data fitting techniques using SciPy for exponential basis functions. Implemented Multivariate Linear Regression models to forecast the influence of varied physical parameters on system energy.
  • Research Innovation: Conducted pioneering experiments to explore geometric frustrations in stressed thin films, bridging the gap between experimental and computational research approaches.
  • Empirical Modeling & Analysis: Systematically collected experimental data for in-depth analysis using OriginPro Lab software. Developed an empirical model to accurately represent the physical behavior of the system under study.
